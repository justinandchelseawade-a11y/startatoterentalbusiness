How to Build a Website for Your Tote Rental Business (Without Paying $1,000)

By Chelsea & Justin Wade — we run a tote rental business and built the site we're about to walk you through.

You don't need a fancy website to rent totes. But you do need a website. The moment someone hears about you, they Google your business name, and what they find decides whether they book or move on. A clean site says "this is a real business." A dead Facebook page or nothing at all says "maybe I'll text them... later."

The good news: you have three realistic ways to get online, and none of them require the $800 to $2,000 a freelancer or agency would quote you. Here's the honest breakdown of each, what your site actually needs, and how to go live this week.

First, what a tote rental website is actually for

It has exactly one job: turn a stranger into a booking. Everything else is decoration. That means a good tote rental site does four things well:

  • Builds trust fast — real photos (or clean illustrations), your service area, and clear pricing so people don't have to ask.
  • Answers the obvious questions — how delivery works, how long a rental lasts, what totes cost.
  • Makes booking effortless — one form, on a phone, that lands straight in your inbox.
  • Shows up on Google — so when someone searches "tote rental near me," your name is there.

If a site does those four things, it's working. If it doesn't, no amount of design polish will save it. Keep that in mind as you weigh the options below.

Option 1: A free website builder (Wix, Carrd, Google Sites)

Best for: someone who genuinely enjoys tinkering and has a free weekend.

Free builders can get you a page with zero dollars down. Carrd is the simplest for a one-page site; Wix and Google Sites give you more room to grow. You drag, you drop, you publish.

The catch is rarely the design — it's everything around it. Free plans usually put the builder's name in your web address (something like yourbusiness.wixsite.com), which quietly undercuts the "real business" impression you're trying to make. To get your own clean address, take payments, and not stare at ads, you end up on a paid plan anyway, usually $15 to $30 a month forever.

If you go this route, budget your time honestly. Most people we talk to spent a full week of evenings and still had the domain and payment setup half-finished.

Option 2: Build it yourself on your own domain

Best for: the DIY-er who wants to own everything and is willing to learn a few new tabs.

This is the "do it right" path. You buy your own domain (around $12 a year through a registrar like GoDaddy or Namecheap), build the pages, connect the domain, set up a business email and a way to take payments, and put it all online. When you're done you own every piece and pay almost nothing monthly.

It's very doable — we wrote free step-by-step guides for the parts that trip people up:

  • Connecting your GoDaddy domain to your site
  • Taking payments with Stripe
  • Setting up a professional business email
  • What pages and features your site actually needs

The honest tradeoff: you'll save the most money and learn the most, but it's the slowest path. The domain-and-DNS step in particular is where almost everyone stalls for a few days.

Option 3: Done-for-you

Best for: anyone who values their time more than the few hundred dollars, or who tried Option 1 or 2 and hit a wall.

This is paying someone to do it for you. The trap here is price: a general freelancer or agency will quote $800 to $2,000+ because they're starting from scratch and have no idea what converts for a tote business. You're paying for their learning curve.

The smarter version is hiring someone who already runs a tote rental business and already has a proven site — so you're getting a finished, booking-ready page for a fraction of agency pricing, not funding an experiment. (That's the gap we built our Launch Package to fill.)

So which should you pick?

Be honest about which of these is really you:

  • You love tech and have time → build it yourself on your own domain. Our guides will get you there.
  • You just want something up today and don't mind a monthly fee → a free builder on a paid plan is fine to start.
  • You want it done right, fast, and off your plate → done-for-you by someone who actually does this.

There's no wrong answer. The only real mistake is letting "I need to build a website" become the thing that keeps you from launching at all. Plenty of tote businesses take their first booking before the site is even perfect.
